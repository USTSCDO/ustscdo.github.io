SEASON OF CREATION

This year, the UST SIMBAHAYAN Community Development Office, in collaboration with the Fellowship for Care of Creation Association, Inc. (FCCAI), Dominican Family for Justice, Peace, and Care for Creation (JPCC) and Alertomas Cluster (Faculty of Arts and Letters, NSTP, Faculty of Engineering, College of Science, College and Architecture and Graduate School), will conduct the Community Service Day remotely in compliance with and in observance of the safety measures in these unprecedented times.

Despite the limitations to conduct community development projects and advocacies at this time, UST SIMBAHAYAN remains resilient in pursuing this annual advocacy activity for UST administrators, faculty, alumni, support staff, and students, with special participation of our partner communities and institutions. As the annual Community Service Day is rooted in environmental advocacies, this year’s virtual Community Service Day continues to support the care of the creation.

OBJECTIVE

SEASON OF CREATION

In the Catholic Bishops’ Conference of the Philippines (CBCP) recent pastoral letter in July 2019, “An Urgent Call for Ecological Conversion, Hope in the Face of Climate Emergency”, the call to celebrate the Season of Creation was re-affirmed as a way of integrating ecological advocacy and the prayer and liturgies of the church.


(Adapted from the Season of Creation 2020: Jubilee for the Earth Concept Note of Global Catholic Climate Movement - Pilipinas)
