SEASON OF CREATION

This year, the UST SIMBAHAYAN Community Development Office, in collaboration with the Office of the Vice-Rector for Religious Affairs, the Fellowship for Care of Creation Association, Inc. (FCCAI), and Dominican Family for Justice, Peace, and Care for Creation, Philippines (DFJPCC-Phils), will conduct an Ecumenism and Interfaith Dialogue remotely in compliance with and in observance of the safety measures in these unprecedented times.

Despite the limitations to conduct community development projects and advocacies at this time, UST SIMBAHAYAN remains resilient in pursuing this advocacy activity for UST administrators, faculty, alumni, support staff, and students, with special participation of our partner communities and institutions. As this closing activity is rooted in environmental advocacies, it aims to provide continued support and awareness to the care of our common home.

Act on this message

OBJECTIVE

To take a proactive response to the call of Pope Francis’ Fratelli Tutti – Fraternity and Social Friendship, by involving the UST administrators, faculty, support staff, and interested alumni in community service

OBJECTIVE

To support the University’s goal of increasing the participation of administrators, faculty, support staff and interested alumni in University-led Community Service Activities
